Hello:
Shifting Visions Films Education Projects Inc. recently screened its first film, “In the Shadow of the Crow: The Legacies of the Narragansetts”. The film focuses on the July 14 2002 smoke shop incident and the Narragansett’s struggle to preserve their sovereignty.

Alexia Kosmider, Producer of the “In the Shadow of the Crow” and the film’s Director, Leslie Langley, were invited to screen their film collaboration at the “Slavery, Genocide and Survival” conference held in March 2005, which was sponsored by the Brown University Steering Committee on Slavery and Justice, Native-to-Native, Native Americans at Brown, and the Center for the Study of Race and Ethnicity department.

The Rhode Island Council of the Humanities, Student Services of University of Rhode Island Feinstein Providence Campus and the Rhode Island Indian Council sponsored two other screenings which were held in April at URI’s Providence Campus and the Rhode Island Indian Council.

Approximately 450 students, Native Americans and other interested persons attended our screenings. In fact, many people have found the film so informative that they have attended more than one screenings; numerous individuals have contacted us via our website (www.shiftingvisons.com) inquiring about future screenings.

We believe that “In the Shadow of the Crow” impacts a broader audience about issues of Native American sovereignty. But like most ‘Indie’ filmmakers, we’ve exhausted our sources for funding. We must now make an appeal to individuals to help sponsor our film until we can locate a film distributor.

A donation of $200 will help us pay licensing fees to the British Museum for photographs used in the film, pay a third of the fee due our sound engineer for audio renovations, or offset costs for film festival expenses. There are many individuals who believe this film is well worth their support.

Donna Mitchell, a Native American and Administrator Manager for Africanus Studies at Brown University, said at the recent Brown screening “Finally a film that shows the Native American perspective.”

Dawn Dove, an active Narragansett tribal member, recounts her experience of watching the film--“There should be more films done like this one.”
Other audience members inquired whether we planned to screen the film on local cable network; and other interested viewers asked if we intended to make the film available internationally. Our Market Consultant, Lynda Martel, is presently contacting HBO and other television venues with this very purpose in mind.

Please consider joining the many individuals who have helped support the vision of this film. We also invite you to visit our website for further information about Shifting Visions and In the Shadow of the Crow— www.shiftingvisons.com

In gratitude of your support of our film project, we will insert the name of your choice (yours, a relative, or a friend) onto our film’s credit list.

Best Wishes,
Alexia Kosmider, Producer, In the Shadow of the Crow
