Alexia Kosmider and Deborah Monuteaux began working together during the production of the In the Shadow of the Crow: Legacies of the Narragansetts. In 2004, with a small crew of dedicated professionals, they formed Shifting Visions, a nonprofit film company with the primary objective of uncovering and bringing to the forefront subjects that they felt needed to be exposed to create a larger dialogue with mainstream society, and with the hopes of bringing sensitivity and understanding to their subjects.

Shifting Vision’s recent documentary, Sappho’s Fire premiered at the Boston LGBTQ Film Festival (2011) at the Museum of Fine Arts. The film has traveled throughout New England and the Northwest, appearing at Women’ Week in Provincetown, Massachusetts, the Rhode Island International Film Festival (2011), the Seattle Gay and Lesbian Film Festival (2011) and the New York LGBTQ Women’s Film Series (2012). After completing Sappho’s Fire, Alexia and Deborah discovered other subjects in the LGBTQ community that they felt were underrepresented as well as misunderstood.

Alexia teaches LGBTQ Literature & Queer Film at RISD, where she has witnessed firsthand the importance of films like Stonewall Uprising is to college students who want to learn about LGBTQ history, or who are discovering their own gender identity. Queer films are a link to the past and future, and are a way for young people to learn about the LGBTQ community; for others, LGBTQ films help them realize that they are not alone, others have come before them.

Alexia and Deborah’s newest film project, TransJourney, also features Deborah’s cousin’s daughter who recently transitioned from a male to a female; the filmmakers therefore have a personal commitment to their new documentary.
